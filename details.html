Why we built this
Story

In many villages, farms and small towns, people still judge water only by how clear it looks, not by simple numbers they can track over time.

Most professional tools need lab reports, strong internet, or advanced English, which can make basic water checks feel distant and complicated.

Water Health Index was created to give students, families and farmers a friendly 0‚Äì100 score, clear Good/Moderate/Poor bands and practical care advice in multiple languages ‚Äì all inside one small website.

Readings stay in your own browser using local storage, so the tool can keep working with low connectivity once it has loaded on your device.

How to use this tool
3‚Äëstep guide

1. Measure

Go to the Measure page, enter pH, temperature and turbidity, or use the camera helper to estimate pH, then save the reading into your history.

2. Explore

Open the Map to see where each reading was taken, and use the Care and Farmers pages to turn the index into clear actions for health and crops.

3. Track

Turn on reminders, repeat tests at the same source and watch whether your water is improving, staying steady or slowly drifting into risk.

What this tool is / is not

  • It is a learning and early‚Äëwarning tool to start conversations about safe water.
  • It is not an official certificate or full chemical analysis ‚Äì heavy metals and germs still need laboratory testing.
  • It cannot replace doctors or health services; if people are already sick, always follow medical advice first.
